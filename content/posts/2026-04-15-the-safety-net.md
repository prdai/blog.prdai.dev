## the conversation

so i was talking to this friend of mine regarding corporate life. and that's when it clicked for me properly.

## first job

i joined my first ever company in december 2024. and it was a crazy experience. i was shit scared on the first day — am i even meant for this? idfk right. but after a while i got used to it.

the work was actually very fun. for the first 6 months i was absolutely shit at it, literally. but towards the end i got "good". not extremely, but good enough. sub par let's say.

in terms of work btw, technically i grew exponentially. it really did push me to see all these smart asf people working. it really is crazy motivating. pushes you every day to see how much these people were able to do in the same time you had. but they did so much more. you really see their impact. and how shit you are. but i guess that is part of how you learn and grow eh.

that job gave me a lot of great memories. made me so much more mature as a person. it was very good.

## the repetitiveness

but then after a few months, after the initial hype died down, it felt so weird. i felt that repetitiveness. it's a curse and the opposite of that at the same time. it's good to have the same thing but it felt so lame. every day standup at this time. that time. this meeting every week. it felt like i was just running on a rat race. the same thing every sprint. first week we'd be chill, second week we'd be cooked, stressed.

it was fun, the people really did make it a great setup. but the work itself felt boring than ever. i was never more motivated to work on other stuff than while i was working. it was fun but boring at the same time.

it felt wrong how everything was so structured. it was like this perfect structure to the world and i felt so out of place with that. a very weird feeling.

## leaving

the work was fun and all that but after i left, which was a very weird decision, i had 3 months. 3 months of just uni and literally back to unemployment type shit.

the first few days, weeks after leaving was so weird. it felt wrong. where is my jira ticket? where is my standup? i literally could not sleep past my standup time because my entire body was built for that lmfao. the most out of place i have ever felt.

then uni started. and that 3 months i got from leaving a very good job — with very good career growth and all that — that 3 months was worth a year of my work. literally the best time of my life. the amount of random side quests. rock climbing. random shit we did. random places we went to. random adventures. it was all worth it. i do not regret leaving for a second.

it feels like it's been years since i left. only it's been a few months. it feels so weird. so wrong.

## the safety net

but all of this is because of my safety net. because i had a safety net to always rely on.

i didn't really need to work. i could've walked out at any point and nothing would have happened. knowing that? that was deeply comforting the entire time.

and honestly, i don't think people appreciate it enough when they're undergraduates or living with their parents. how free it is. literally in terms of freedom but like expenses. there is nothing to be spent on.

i think about people around me who don't have that. who actually need the paycheck. the job hits different when leaving isn't an option.

## the fear of losing it

i was planning to go to japan this may right. but i'm not going. partly because i don't wanna and some bullshit i don't wanna get into. but also partly because if i was to go, i would have to work. i would have to work to exist.

existing for me right now is like baseline. i get my food. i got a roof under my head. i'm all gucci. i'm very happy. but having to rely on it. i do not wanna imagine.

i will have to get to that point, i know. but i'm really scared of getting to that point right now. that safety net means a lot. and i'm not everyone can do this. i'm thankful that i can. it's very comforting knowing that.

i'm going to a new company this june if they don't terminate my job before that lmao. but it's gonna be fun. i think it's gonna be better. it's gonna be a lot that i will learn.

but i'm scared for the day that i will not have it anymore. where you have to care about taxes and shit. rent. food. how expensive it gets. how reliant you become on that paycheck. specially if you have a family or something.

when you get to a point where you have a family and all that and you really can't be asked to give a fuck about what you work on and you care about that paycheck. that also scares me. to work on something i do not like just for the sake of the money.

## the exchange

it's not a bad thing. employment is an exchange of your time for money. but you want to feel like it is more than that. i wanted to when i started my job initially. but slowly towards the end it felt like that was all it was. honestly just an exchange of money for your time. i guess that's how those tax paying adults feel huh.

i hope not to get fed up with my job. that's what i want. not to feel or not to come to that point where i feel that exchange of money and time. that is how you know you are really enjoying your work i guess. that's what i felt in the beginning.

these 3 months have been great. and i'm scared that i might not be able to do this again without my safety net. i want this freedom i had. no sleep schedules. no one to tell me what the fuck to do. go and come any time. this freedom. this chillness. all gone with employment.

i'm not sure how i will end up. but this is a weird point i'm at. right now, work is such a big priority. but if it does not become it — if the safety net just disappears — how do i make it work? it feels like a lot of unknowns. hopefully i will deal with it as it comes.

but for now i guess i just gotta live in my lil safety net huh. :))
