i hate the word "prodigy." that's probably the best way to put what i'm trying to talk about though, so here we are.

i was that kid. the one who was always building something, always learning something, constantly working. and the stuff i did back then — that was the thing. that's what actually set things up.

and it carries into today. directly. like, things i can do now, i can do because of work i put in when i was way too young to be doing it.

## the cost

but here's the thing. i feel like shit when i'm not productive. disproportionately bad. and i know exactly why — because back then my baseline was running at like full output all the time. that became normal. so now when i'm not at that level, something feels wrong even when it isn't.

## the visibility thing

i wasn't the best. there were people better than me, more technically impressive. but i had visibility they didn't. i got advantages from that. got pulled into things. and it helped, genuinely.

but i also hated it. at the same time.

that's the weird mix. the advantages were real and i hated that they were handed to me the way they were. grateful and resentful in the same breath.

still sitting with it. :)
