i hate the word "prodigy." genuinely hate it. but i also don't know a better one for what i'm trying to talk about here, so we're just gonna go with it and i'll resent it the whole time.

## that kid

i was one of those kids. the ones where adults would do the weird look-at-each-other thing when you'd answer a question you weren't supposed to know. the ones who'd get dragged out at family events to show people stuff. the ones who had a "thing."

and i did the work. constantly. that's the part people always leave out of these stories, the framing makes it sound like it was some kind of magic that just fell out of me. it wasn't. i was grinding. every day. reading things i didn't fully understand, trying to build stuff that was way above my level, failing, going again, failing again. that was my baseline for years on end.

and i'm not saying any of this to flex. i say it because it's the part that matters for the rest of this.

## the gift

here's the thing nobody tells you about that kind of childhood. it carries you. for a really long time. way past the age where you stopped being "that kid."

almost everything i know how to do now, i know because of something i started messing with when i was way too young to be messing with it. the instincts are from back then. the pattern recognition is from back then. the fact that i can sit down in front of a new tool and not flinch is from back then too. i'm basically cashing checks i wrote for myself when i was twelve.

that's the real advantage. not the visibility, not the "omg he's so smart" stuff. the compounding. i got a head start nobody can really give you retroactively, and a decade later i'm still using it.

## the cost

but.

there is a cost and it's actually brutal.

i feel like absolute shit when i'm not being productive. not in a normal way. disproportionately bad. a day where i didn't build something, didn't learn something, didn't push forward on anything at all? my brain files that under "moral failure" and sends me the bill.

and i know exactly where that comes from. it comes from spending an entire childhood running at like 180% output. that became the baseline. that became "normal." so now when i'm operating at a regular, sustainable human pace, which is literally fine and how everyone else lives, it feels to me like i'm rotting in place.

this is not a healthy way to exist. i know that. i'm working on it. but knowing where a problem comes from doesn't switch it off. the standard is set. the brain remembers the standard.

## the visibility thing

the other part of being "that kid" was the visibility. and this is where it gets weird because i genuinely hated it and genuinely benefited from it at the same time.

i wasn't the best. i want to be clear about that. there were always people better than me. technically sharper, more impressive, doing wilder things. but for whatever reason, i had visibility they didn't. i got pulled into stuff. i got introduced to people. opportunities landed in my lap because someone remembered me from some event i did three years ago.

and that stuff compounds too. every opportunity leads to two more. every door someone opens for you makes the next door lighter. that's just how it works once you're in the loop.

but god, it felt weird. because i knew the equation wasn't clean. i knew there were people grinding harder than me who weren't getting pulled into those same rooms. and that gap between effort and recognition is one of those things you either let eat you alive or you learn to sit with. i mostly chose to sit with it. mostly.

## the weird mix

so that's where i am with it. grateful and resentful in the same breath.

the childhood that set me up is the same childhood that installed a permanent "you're not doing enough" voice in my head. the visibility that opened doors is the same visibility that made me feel like a fraud every time i walked through one. i can't really take one half of it without the other. it's a package deal.

i don't know how to resolve any of this and i don't really think i'm supposed to. it's just the shape of it. you take the whole thing or you don't take it at all.

but yeah. i still hate the word. :)
